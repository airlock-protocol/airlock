What just happened

Money became something an agent can spend.

On 20 February 2026, Razorpay and NPCI launched agentic UPI payments on Anthropic’s Claude at the India AI Impact Summit — Zomato, Swiggy and Zepto live in the pilot. An AI agent now completes the journey from discovery to checkout inside a single conversation.

It runs on UPI Reserve Pay: a user pre-authorizes a spending limit per merchant, and the agent transacts within it — no PIN per purchase. For the first time at national scale, an AI agent holds standing permission to move real money.

The gap

A spending limit isn’t a decision.

Reserve Pay caps how much an agent can spend. It doesn’t decide whether this action, right now, is the one the user intended. A pre-authorized agent that reads an untrusted input — a poisoned product listing, a crafted merchant reply — can be steered to spend inside its own authorization.

And a probabilistic model can’t guarantee it won’t. That’s the exact seam RBI’s FREE-AI framework points at: provable authorization and auditable records for AI in finance. That’s a job for policy, not a prompt.

Built for India’s rules

Designed for what FREE-AI is built around.

RBI’s Framework for Responsible & Ethical Enablement of AI centres on how transactions are authorised, validated and audited. Airlock produces exactly that evidence.

Deterministic authorization

Every agent action checked against policy before it executes — same input, same verdict, independent of the model.

Tamper-evident audit

Ed25519-signed, hash-chained receipts. Alter one and the chain breaks — the record that survives a supervisory review.

Independently verifiable

Anyone can check a receipt’s signature and chain — no trust in Airlock required. Fit for a National Repository of audited decisions.

Model- & provider-agnostic

Works across Claude, other models, and your own agents. The authorization boundary is deterministic even when the agent isn’t.

Airlock is designed to produce the authorization and audit evidence FREE-AI is built around. It is not a certification or a claim of RBI endorsement.
